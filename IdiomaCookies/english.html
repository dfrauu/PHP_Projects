Guitar History

The Spanish guitar or creole guitar, as it is known in some places in Latin America, is a musical instrument of the chordophone family with a very old origin but which begins to have a very remarkable importance after the Renaissance. It was at this time that the contrapuntal repertoire of plucked string instruments was developed: of the vihuela in Spain and America and of the lute in the rest of Europe, which gave rise to the classical guitar.
